High-Dollar Invoice Approval — verifiable on-chain

A BizFirst workflow routes an invoice through a governance-based approval matrix, then anchors the decision on the Accumulate mainnet as a tamper-evident record. Anyone can verify it — no trust required.

How it works

1 · IntakeInvoice submitted (vendor, amount).
2 · ClassifyAI picks the approval tier by amount.
3 · ApproveApprovalNode routes N-of-M sign-off.
4 · AnchorDecision hashed & written to Accumulate.
5 · VerifyAnyone recomputes the hash & checks the chain.

Data account (live): acc://marcdebary.acme/receipts

Demo approvals — three tiers

InvoiceAmountTier · ruleDecision receipt (SHA-256)

Each receipt is the SHA-256 of the canonical decision JSON, written on-chain as a DoubleHash entry.

Verify it yourself

Pick a demo decision (or paste your own), then compute the hash in your browser. This is exactly what the anchor writes — recompute it and you have proof the decision is authentic and unaltered.

Canonicalization: object keys sorted recursively, arrays kept in order, then SHA-256 of the UTF-8 bytes. Reference implementation and test vectors: 005.ReferenceImplementations/InvoiceApproval. Interim JavaScript anchor; the native Accumulate C# node is in progress. · DE BARY LLC.
